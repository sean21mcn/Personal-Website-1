David Sean McNeeley, Tenor

From a young age, David Sean McNeeley had a distinct talent for music. Picking up various instruments along the way, chief among them being the violin, oboe and trombone, with his distinct musicianship he was able to attend the Saint Thomas Choir School in New York City. There, as a part of the Saint Thomas Choir of Men and Boys he honed his craft under the tutelage of Dr. Gerre Hancock and Dr. John Scott before attending the Juilliard School's Pre-College Division.

Sean went on to obtain his Bachelor in Music from the Eastman School of Music in Rochester, NY, where he branched out as a performer. In the Rochester area, Sean has performed as a soloist for Eastman’s Bach Cantata series, Beethoven’s Mass in C, Haydn’s Mass in C and the Messiah. In the world of opera, Sean has performed the role of Simon Stimson in Ned Rorem’s Our Town, as well as excerpts of Belmonte in Der Entführung aus dem Serail, Basilio in Le Nozze di Figaro, Ferrando in Così fan tutte, Nemorino in L’elisir d’amore, Ubaldo in Armida, Pong in Turandot and Dr. Cajus in Falstaff.

In recent years, Sean has also been fortunate to study under such maestros as Lorraine Nubar, Robert Swensen, Benton Hess, Chris Merritt, Nicole Cabell, Håkan Hagegård, Federico Sacchi, Nicholas Clapton, Mira Zakai, Hugh Murphy and Enza Ferrari.
